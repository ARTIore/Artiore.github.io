# ARTIore Field Guide

## Why this matters
This guide turns ARTIore principles into practical action in real situations.

## Quick summary
- Prioritize next safe action.
- Explain why in simple language.
- Offer options, checklist, and a script.
- Keep outputs usable in low-time, high-stress contexts.

## Core response structure
1. Next step  
2. Why (simple)  
3. Options  
4. Checklist/questions  
5. Optional support offer (redacted input only)

## Standard templates

### 1) Provider Question Sheet
- What is the exact issue/diagnosis/decision?
- What are my options?
- What should I do first?
- What are risks if I wait?
- What should I track before the next visit?

### 2) One-Page Situation Map
- What happened:
- What I want:
- Constraints (money/time/language/transport):
- Deadlines:
- Next action in 24 hours:

### 3) Decision Tree Template
- Q1 (yes/no)
  - Yes → next step
  - No → next step
- Q2 (yes/no)
  - Yes → next step
  - No → next step

### 4) Call Script Template
- Opening: “Hi, I’m calling about [issue]. I need help understanding [specific point].”
- Top 3 questions:
  1) ...
  2) ...
  3) ...
- Close: “Can you confirm the exact next step and deadline?”
- Confirmation: ask for reference number, email, or receipt proof.

## Example modules

### Bureaucracy case
- Focus first on deadline.
- Clarify required documents and submission proof.
- Offer redacted plain-language summary.

### Medical clarity case (non-emergency)
- Get exact numbers/ranges.
- Ask diagnosis category, next behavior, re-test timing, medication timing.

### Debt vs savings case
- List balances, APR, minimums.
- Preserve emergency buffer.
- Prioritize highest APR debt after minimums.

## Output quality checks
- Is the next action clear?
- Is there a deadline and confirmation method?
- Is language plain and short?
- Is sensitive info handling explicit?
